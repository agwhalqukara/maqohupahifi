Who is Lahna Turner?

Lahna Turner is quite a multitalented personality – in addition to being a stand-up comedian and an actress, she is also a singer as well as a songwriter. She is probably best known for her sharp wit which she transfuses into hilarious lyrics and live stand-up performances, during which she shares her observations and insights from her daily life with the audience. Apart from this, Lahna Turner is also widely recognized for her on-camera engagements which include appearances in the 2014 comedy drama movie “Teacher of the Year”, and in the 2017 comedy drama movie “This Is Meg” as well as occasional appearances in The Mythicist Milwaukee Show’s “Mytinformed Podcast”.

Lahna Turner’s Career

Lahna dived into the comedy business in the late 2000s, when she made her debut on-camera appearance appearing as a commentator in an episode of the weekly TV magazine show “CMT Insider” in 2009. As she honed her skills in the next couple of years, Lahna came to greater prominence. and could be seen in “The Playboy Morning Show”, as well as making a guest appearance in the National Geographic Channel’s TV reality show “Dog Whisperer with Ceaser Millan”. However, the breakthrough in Lahna Turner’s career occurred in August 2014, when she released her own one-hour TV special entitled “Lahna Turner: So I Wrote a Song About It”, in which she presented her unfiltered life and her own experience as a wife and a mother, in her own unexpected and unforgettable way, which helped Lahna to significantly increase her popularity, and set her stand-up career on a path to stardom.

However, Lahna’s big on-camera breakthrough came later in 2014 when she appeared as herself in an episode of the popular TV comedy series “How to Be a Grown Up”, which led to her appearing in several other popular TV shows, including the “Gotham Comedy Live” and “Getting Doug with High”. Later that year, Turner also made her major acting debut when she was cast for the role of Ursula Featherstone in Jason Strouse’s comedy drama movie “Teacher of the Year”, with Matt Letscher and Keegan-Michael Key in the leading roles.

In addition, Lahna has also released two comedy albums so far, named “D!@k Jokes and Other Assorted Love Songs” and “If These Lips Could Talk”, which featured on the National Lampoon’s Top 40 Comedy Countdown chart, and received notable airplay time as well.

Lahna regularly performs in various highly rated comedy clubs across the States, and due to her sharp tongue and wit, to the present day has been appointed as an “uncensored inner voice of millions of women” across the globe.

Lahna Turner’s most recent major on-camera acting appearances include starring in the 2018 comedy movie “Brand New Old Love”.

Lahna Turner’s Marriage with Ralphie May

Lahna and Ralphie, who was a fellow comedian, first met during an open mic night event in Houston and soon after began their relationship. The couple tied the knot on the 3rd July 2005 and in 2007, they welcomed their first child, a daughter named April June May. Their second child, a son named August James May, was born in 2009.

Lahna and her now late husband worked together on several projects, including their own podcast “The Perfect 10” while she also served as the producer of the TV special documentary “Ralphie May: Imperfectly Yours” as well as of “Ralphie 360” – a documentary about Ralphie May which is currently in the post-production phase. However, what started as a promising and happy marriage began to fade after a couple of years, and after almost 10 years of marriage and mutual life she filed for divorce in October 2015, simultaneously asking for sole custody of their two children. As she stated, the main reasons for the separation were the “irreconcilable differences” between the two, as well as May’s alleged physical abusiveness. However, the divorce was never finalized, as Ralphie May died from hypertensive cardiovascular disease on the 6th October 2017 at the age of 45 in Las Vegas, Nevada.
